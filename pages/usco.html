project [URBAN STERLING]

    OVERVIEW

    Urban Sterling is a small business trading in silver jewellery, predominantly rings. Although, the collection is expanding to include pendants and chains as well as, earrings. Mason Mignianelli, the founder and one man team of Urban Sterling designs and produces almost all of his designs. This design project proposal aims to outline a plan for a redesign of the website.

    TOOLS

  • Figma
  • Adobe Photoshop
  • Adobe InDesign
  • Visual Studio Code
    CHALLENGES

  • Poor hierarchy
  • Poor readability
  • Dated design
  • Inappropriate naming of labels
  • Too many endpoints/pages
  • Overlapping elements creating clutter
  • Too many moving objects generating fatigue
  • Lack of colour
    PROJECT GOALS

  • Future proof
  • Appear more reliable
  • Interesting interaction pattern
  • Reduce pain points
  • Re-enforce branding
    TARGET AUDIENCE

  • 18-30 year olds
  • Creatives who celebrate art and culture
  • People who value old traditional techniques i.e. lost wax casting
  • Interested in supporting local small businesses
  • Value wearable art
  • Value artists' desire to tell a story
    PROPOSED CONCEPT

    Rather than focusing solely on a particular demographic, we want to lean more into a psychographic as well. In context to this particular redesign, since jewellery is something that can appeal to a large variety of people, and has more to do with what speaks to them than what any particular organisation decides to sell to them. Keeping a large audience and making work that can be considered unisex is something Mason wants to focus on quite heavily so here, we want to be decisive but still broad.

    We wish to introduce a brand colour as well as transition towards soft UI to modernise the look and feel of the website. A more bold interface leveraging large typography, hero images and a more impactful landing screen should be introduced to pull the user in immediately. Micro-animations should be included to make the user experience a bit more dynamic without detracting from the subject matter as well as the end goal of making a sale.
